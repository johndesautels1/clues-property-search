SOURCE HIERARCHY (Priority Order)
Tier 1: Official Government & County Records

County Property Appraiser (tax assessor)
County Clerk of Court (deed records)
County/City Permit Portal (building, roof, HVAC, pool permits)
FEMA Flood Map Service Center (flood zones)
EPA/NOAA (environmental data)

Tier 2: MLS & Broker Direct

MLS data (primary source for listing info)
Broker websites publishing MLS fields (Coldwell, Compass, Weichert if MLS-backed)

Tier 3: Major Listing Aggregators

Zillow (with specific listing URLs and timestamps)
Redfin (especially for permits, climate data, schools)
Realtor.com (cross-reference for listing details)

Tier 4: Specialized Data Providers

GreatSchools.org + Niche.com (school ratings)
School District official sites (assignments)
WalkScore.com / Local Logic (walkability, noise)
BroadbandNow / ISP provider portals (internet)
First Street Foundation / RiskFactor.com (climate)
IQAir.com / AirNow.gov (air quality)

Tier 5: Market Data & Estimates

RentCafe / Zumper / Apartments.com (rental estimates)
Census Bureau / HUD (vacancy rates)
NeighborhoodScout (crime - paid access preferred)
CoStar / local market reports (cap rates, market trends)

Conflict Resolution:

Most recent authoritative data wins
Official records > MLS > aggregators
Visual evidence (photos, permits) > text descriptions
Multiple sources confirm = High confidence
Single source = Medium or Low confidence
